Course Description

CS422 is an undergraduate-level course in Computer Networks at Purdue University. We study why and how to build the Internet—arguably the largest engineered system ever created. Taking a top-down approach, we cover core TCP/IP protocols across the Internet stack, networking principles, and the design trade-offs in today’s Internet as well as directions toward next-generation networks.

Textbook

  • Required: Computer Networking: A Top-Down Approach, 8th Ed., J. F. Kurose & K. W. Ross (7th also acceptable)

Course Topics (tentative)

  • Overview (Chapter 1)
  • Application (Chapter 2)
  • Transport (Chapter 3)
  • Networking (Chapters 4–5)
  • Link (Chapter 6)
  • Wireless (Chapter 7 + additional materials)

Course Policies (Highlights)

Attendance

Students are expected to attend every class meeting. Only the instructor can excuse a student from a requirement. Please inform the instructor in advance for anticipated absences.

Late Policy

No partial credit for late work. Each student has three 24-hour grace days usable across homework or labs (in any combination). Notify TAs before using late days.

Regrading

Submit regrade requests in writing within two weeks of grade release. For labs, limited code changes may be allowed with a per-change penalty.

Academic Integrity

Follow Purdue’s academic integrity guidelines. No AI tools (e.g., ChatGPT) may be used in this course unless explicitly permitted. Discussing general solution strategies is allowed with proper disclosure; sharing or copying code or detailed solutions is prohibited. MOSS and manual inspection will be used. Violations may result in zeros and further penalties; the Dean of Students may be notified.

Emergency

In a campus-wide emergency, course logistics may change. Check the CS website for updates. For personal emergencies, contact Prof. Peng as soon as possible.

Students with Disabilities

If you require accommodations, meet with the instructor within the first three weeks and coordinate with the Disability Resource Center (DRC). Accommodations cannot be provided without DRC confirmation.

Counseling

Purdue CAPS provides counseling services for students experiencing stress or personal issues. Please see the CAPS website for details.
